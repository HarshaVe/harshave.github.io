About

Harsha, who is the “Harsha” and the “V” part of HarshaV.com, is currently the Head of Design for Robert Bosch North America. In this capacity, he is working to build on Bosch's excellence as a leading global supplier of technology and services by driving adoption of innovative, customer-driven, design-thinking solutions and services.

Before joining Robert Bosch, Harsha was the User Experience Manager for General Motors Global Connected Customer Experience team. He lead web, device, wearable experience design and overall testing efforts, focused on developing extraordinary designers and delivering remarkable designs.

Prior to joining GM, Harsha contracted to the United States Air Force's Air Mobility Command (USAF AMC), where he lead the design and development of a suite of logistics and communications software tools, and developed command-wide experience and style guidelines.

Harsha's agency and contracting experience started before the USAF when he served as Analytics and User Experience Manager at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. There he led Analytics and UX efforts in building and supporting mission critical, award-winning sites and applications.

Harsha has also worked in a variety of UX, Product Management and development roles across industries such as non-profit, education, news/media, and energy trading.

Like The Ultimate Warrior, Harsha hails from parts unknown. He would also prefer to be introduced as "weight unknown" and "height unknown."

myChevrolet

visual design, interaction design design, user research, leadership, service revenue, mobile

Situation

In 2016, General Motors (GM) was spending millions of dollars a year on a collection of limiting, and fractured mobile apps. Despite the effort and resources directed towards the mobile channel, the apps were failing to serve owner needs or drive owner engagement. The failure of the apps to serve owners also limited the brands (Chevrolet, Buick, GMC, Cadillac, Opel and Vauxhall) relevance to in-vehicle interactions.

Compounding matters, vehicles sales volumes were shrinking, making it critical for GM to find additional revenue streams. After all, GM sold a few million new cars a year, but there were ten times as many GM vehicle owners with older cars.

Finding a way to be of service to them could generate millions in revenue, but apps that were deleted after a single use would never get us there.

Task

I was brought in to manage the User Experience teams responsible for one of the mobile apps, as well as the desktop (responsive) sites and a few other products such Android Auto and Apple’s CarPlay integration.

Our challenge was to produce a mobile app that:

  • Reduced our production costs
  • Expanded our connected vehicle services (OnStar) globally
  • Provide a platform to realize new service- driven revenue streams

Of course none of that could happen if we kept getting deleted from phones, so job one was to create an experience with the app that owners valued and engaged with.
